Strengths of the Original Plan

  • It starts with a clear base case and probabilities, which is the right mental model for a daily rates dashboard.
  • The market confirmation grid encourages cross-asset validation instead of staring only at the 30Y yield.
  • The hypothesis scorecard and invalidation rules introduce discipline and reduce narrative drift.
  • The catalyst list correctly focuses attention on CPI, payrolls, Fed pricing, auctions, volatility, and curve behavior.

Weaknesses to Fix

  • The plan does not define how probabilities are calculated, so the headline outlook can become subjective decoration.
  • It mixes cyclical growth, inflation, fiscal supply, technicals, and risk appetite without ranking their importance for the 30Y point.
  • It lacks a source hierarchy, freshness checks, and a distinction between market-implied data and analyst judgment.
  • It misses term premium, real yield decomposition, auction concession/tails, positioning, and global long-end spillovers.
  • It needs an audit trail showing which signal moved since yesterday and why the scenario odds changed.

Improved Build Specification

  • Use a two-layer model: hard inputs from market/macro sources, then an explicit judgment overlay for regime and data quality.
  • Make the default page answer three questions in order: where is the yield, what is driving it, and which scenario gained evidence?
  • Track six factor families: Fed path, inflation/real yields, growth risk, term premium/fiscal supply, risk appetite/liquidity, and technicals/positioning.
  • Every alert should map to an action: upgrade upside break risk, downgrade rally risk, check source quality, or wait for a confirming close.
  • Keep daily notes beside the scorecard so the interface becomes a research log, not just a screen of gauges.

Probability Methodology

Scenario probabilities are model-implied weights from technical, real-rate, breakeven, curve and risk-appetite factors. They are a transparent decision aid, not calibrated investment forecasts yet.

This local build uses refreshed FRED data and Yahoo market proxies when available. It should not be treated as investment advice or a live market-data terminal until the refresh process and factor model are reviewed against your preferred source set.

Valuation and market inputs can change quickly. Verify live data before using this dashboard for investment decisions.
